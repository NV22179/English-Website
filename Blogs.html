NV22132 - Jassim Mohammed

As a student at NCST, I began using AI because my friends mentioned it to me. Initially, I used it to find answers to my questions and to learn more about school subjects. AI turned out to be really helpful, especially when I was studying for exams, as it helped me review things more quickly by explaining them in a way that made sense to me. However, when I first started using AI, it wasn’t as advanced as it is now, and I sometimes encountered wrong information. Despite these early challenges, I quickly saw the potential of AI in enhancing my learning experience.

Currently, I use ChatGPT for general questions, Claude for its accuracy and speed, and NoteGPT to extract notes from YouTube videos, which helps me save time and manage my studies better. These tools have really helped boost my grades by making studying more effective. I’ve realized that AI is there to support me, not to do the work for me, so I use it for ideas and direction instead of just copying what it says. This approach has made me more independent and confident in my studies.

For instance, when I was working on the Pearson report, I turned to ChatGPT for some creative ideas to finish it quicker and make sure it was well-written. The suggestions I received helped me structure my report more effectively and add unique insights that I might not have thought of on my own. This experience showed me how AI can be a valuable tool for enhancing my academic work.

All in all, AI has been a fantastic help in my education, significantly changing how I learn and succeed. It has not only improved my grades but also made learning more enjoyable and less stressful. By integrating AI into my study routine, I feel better prepared for my future career and more equipped to tackle complex challenges.

NV22090 - Ali Jaffer

NCST is actively fighting deforestation and pollution through sustainable learning practices. By conducting tests and assignments online using laptops or iPads instead of paper, we significantly reduce paper waste. This change has a profound impact on the environment. Imagine 500 students using more than three papers each day; that's a substantial amount of paper, which translates to many trees. Trees are crucial for our environment as they absorb carbon dioxide, release oxygen, and maintain biodiversity. Preserving trees is essential, and this digital shift helps achieve that.

Conducting online tests and assignments also lowers waste significantly. Paper waste often ends up in landfills or is incinerated, releasing harmful chemicals into the air. By reducing paper usage, we cut down on this environmental impact. Additionally, using technology for assignments and tests is more convenient, eliminating concerns about misplaced papers or running out of pages, as everything is stored on one device.

While using technology might not be easy for everyone, it encourages learning and understanding of modern devices, which is crucial as digital work is the future. Although technical issues can arise, they can be resolved by experts, and the environmental benefits far outweigh these minor inconveniences.

By making this shift, NCST is not only helping the environment but also setting an example for others to follow. This small change by one school can contribute to larger-scale changes, promoting a healthier planet.
